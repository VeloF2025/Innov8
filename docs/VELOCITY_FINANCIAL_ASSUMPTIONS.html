üí∞ Revenue Model & Pricing

Base Revenue Assumption
Daily Subscription Fee per Active ONT:
R10.00 (mature rate)

Source: Conservative pricing for township markets. Comparable to R300/month unlimited data service (R10/day equivalent). Accounts for local market willingness-to-pay and competition from satellite providers.

Tiered Pricing Model (Revenue Ramp)

Unlike instant full-price revenue, we model gradual pricing acceleration as projects mature and network credibility increases:

Monthly Revenue Calculation: Active ONTs √ó Days in Month √ó Price per ONT per Day

Monthly OPEX Structure
Hub-Based OPEX (per project):
R400,000/month (Y1-Y2)

Includes: Regional management (50%), technical operations (30%), customer service allocation (15%), general allocation (5%). Based on Hub & Spoke model serving 4-5 projects per hub.

OPEX Scaling & Hub & Spoke Efficiency

As portfolio grows, per-project OPEX declines through shared infrastructure:

Period Portfolio Scale Per-Project OPEX Efficiency Gain
Year 1 4 projects R400K/month --
Year 2 12 projects (3 hubs) R360K/month -10% (shared management)
Year 3 24 projects (6 hubs) R320K/month -20% (regional optimization)
Mature 24+ projects R320K/month Stable (44% below Y1)
Why Hub & Spoke Works: One regional manager supervises 4-5 projects. One customer service center handles 4-5 projects. One technical team manages infrastructure across hub. This eliminates per-project overhead while maintaining quality.
Capitalized Costs (Not in Monthly OPEX)
  • Fiber Infrastructure: R13.5M (10,000 doors, 20km fiber, GPON equipment)
  • ONT Termination: Included in setup, amortized across 7+ year lifecycle
  • Network Operations Center: One-time setup amortized across hub
  • Maintenance Reserve: Embedded in monthly OPEX (5% of revenue post-ramp)

‚úÖ Assumptions Validation & Sources

Conservative Positioning
  • Pricing (R10/day): Below metro fiber (R500+/month) but above satellite (R200-400/month). Comparable to Vumatel's B-segment pricing.
  • Activation Rate (250-300/week): Achievable with 2-person field teams, township community engagement practices.
  • Penetration Cap (80%): FNO industry standard. Vumatel, Openserve typically see 75-85% in mature markets.
  • OPEX (R400K/month): Based on sector benchmarks; includes margin for contractor payment variations.
  • Tiered Pricing Ramp: Reflects reality of customer acquisition across growth phases, not instant full pricing.
Why Not Optimistic Scenarios?

Our model deliberately avoids "best case" assumptions like:

  • Instant 90%+ penetration (unrealistic in township markets)
  • R10/day pricing from Day 1 (customer acquisition requires incentives)
  • Single-project maturity in Year 1 (portfolio takes time to ramp)
  • Zero churn (assumes 100% retention, but we assume 100% new activations)

üìñ Glossary of Terms

ONT (Optical Network Termination)

The device installed at customer premises that terminates the fiber network and converts light signals to electrical signals for broadband use. Cost: ~R6K per unit.

Penetration Rate

Percentage of homes passed (R.O.U.) that have active ONTs with paying subscriptions. 80% penetration means 8,000 of 10,000 homes have service.

Hub & Spoke Architecture

Centralized management model where one regional "hub" (office) manages 4-5 fiber network "spokes" (projects). Reduces per-project overhead through shared management and operations teams.

OPEX (Operating Expenditure)

Monthly operational costs including salaries, vehicle maintenance, customer service, NOC operations, and general overhead. Excludes capex (fiber, equipment).

Operational Breakeven

The month when monthly revenue exceeds monthly OPEX for the first time. After this point, project generates positive monthly cash flow (but may not yet achieve cumulative payback).

Cumulative Payback Period

The month when cumulative cash flow (revenues minus OPEX) equals initial capital investment. Accounts for entire startup period including setup phase losses.

Annual Yield / ROI

Annual cash flow divided by initial investment, expressed as percentage. If project generates R96M/year with R135M investment, yield = 71.1%.

Blended Portfolio Yield

Weighted average yield across all projects in portfolio, accounting for staggered launches. Realistic measure of investor returns across entire portfolio.

‚ùì Common Questions

Q: Why is Year 1 yield (34.5%) so much lower than mature project yield (71.1%)?

A: Year 1 has 4 projects at different maturity stages. First project (launched Month 1) reaches 71% yield by Month 8. But projects 2, 3, 4 (launched Months 2-4) are just beginning ramp. Blended across all four, Year 1 average is 34.5%. This is realistic, not optimistic.

Q: What if our activation rate is slower than 1,100/month?

A: Projects hitting 50% penetration (5K ONTs) still yield 32.6%, which is attractive. We model 1,100/month as achievable with proven field team model. If slower, project still works, just takes longer and cumulative portfolio grows more slowly.

Q: Why does OPEX stay at R400K even as ONTs grow from 1K to 8K?

A: Hub model: one manager oversees project regardless of size. One customer service team handles 1K-8K ONTs with minimal marginal cost. As hub grows to 5 projects, fixed costs spread. Per-project OPEX does decline as portfolio scales (R400K ‚Üí R360K ‚Üí R320K).

Q: Is R10/day realistic for township markets?

A: Yes. Equivalent to R300/month for unlimited data. Satellite (Starlink) costs R500+. Fiber should command premium. We reach R10 only in Month 9+ (mature phase), starting at R3.50 for early adopters. This pricing ladder is realistic and proven in FNO markets.

Q: What happens to revenue after 10 months (beyond ramp)?

A: Projects mature at 8K ONTs. Monthly revenue flattens at R2.4M (8K √ó 30 √ó R10). Growth comes from: (1) new projects in portfolio expanding overall revenue, (2) slight price increases over time, (3) additional revenue streams (corporate accounts, managed services) not modeled conservatively.
